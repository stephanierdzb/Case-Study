Research


When creating the first survey I focused on if the user had any previous knowledge of this organization. We also wanted to learn if the message was clear in their homepage, and how easy/ahd it was to use the desktop and mobile versions. After our first survey results were analyzed we knew we had to make a few iterations. For one, alot of the survey was not Quantative Data, but more written and personal opinions. Our second iteration included more upfront questions, yes/no answers and ratings.

Q1: Do you have friends in or family that serve(d) in the military?
Q2: Would you donate to a organization that host Outdoor Recreation Events Annually to Military Warriors?
Q4: Would you donate to an organizstion that has awarded 130 Payment Free Vehicles to Military Warriors & Gold Star Families?
Q5: Would you be willing to attend a Outdoor Recreation Ecent that host over 300 military warriors?
Q6: How clear do they communicate their message?
Q7: How much would you donate a month to this orgazniation?

And lastly, the placeholder content for the third and final accordion panel. This panel is hidden by default.

Wireframe


These are my original Low-Fidelity Wireframs
I really wanted the mission statement to be one of the first things the user sees, and by incorporating a photo with the mission statement, I thought this would give it more legitimacy and value.
For the programs I wanted to be able to read a small description of each one, and have a button to learn more about each one of them.
I wanted to add a space dedicated to the News Articles they are featured in, again to establish that trust, and to also add a testimonials our users can see that they do give all these awards.
Lastly, I wanted to lock in that trust by having a place for their awards.
